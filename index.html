Who We Are

We are a group of design driven individuals passionate about creating a solution for the space debris problem.

MyNightSky is a start-up working to aid in the removal of space debris through the design and manufacturing of a fully functional satellite. In accordance to the European Space Agency’s Clean Space Initiative, our team is working towards a cleaner and more accessible space by involving ourselves in the mitigation and removal process.

Problem & Solution

The mess is great but our plans to save space are even greater.

We are a group of multidisciplinary students working together to alleviate an issue that becomes more and more prominent as space launches and payloads become very accessible to the public. Anybody can send something up—the issue is bringing it back down.

Define

Before 2008, a total of about 4,600 launches have occurred, placing nearly 6000 satellites into orbit. Out of these 6000 spacecraft, only 800 are still operational today. This means that 85% of the bodies sent up into orbit, do not even serve a function to us here on Earth. To add to this matter, including the 800 tracked, functional satellites, there are more than 22,000 pieces larger than 4 inches, which are being tracked by the U.S. Space Surveillance Network. We could also add in the 500,000 fragments less than four inches, but greater than .4in estimated to be whipping around in orbit alongside the first 22,000, and yet still millions of particles less than that .4in measurement.

Something needs to be done. We can be this something.

Design

Our company plans on increasing the efficiency of laser ablation to use as both a propulsion method, as well as a debris removal method. Our initial plan is to create an ablative laser propulsion system and build a smallsat around that. There are many benefits to using laser propulsion, and if the thrust can be increased, we could revolutionize the industry.

Build

We are currently prototyping this system. Beginning primarily with the laser propulsion system before moving into the laser system used for ablating debris.
